1 Billion Bits

Our mission

The growing availability of gigabit fiber networks has prompted many of the world's best technical minds to ask, "Just what is possible with that much bandwidth?" While an incredible technological advance in its own right, gigabit internet is just infrastructure. The foundation on which the real advances that will change people's lives and create the future are going to be built.

The sponsors and organizers of 1 Billion Bits want to help answer those questions and raise new ones by putting engineers, researchers, and enthusiasts interested in Gigabit Internet & Applications in the same room. 1 Billion Bits is that room. Where once every month CTO's, inventors, engineers, and developers working to create the future can come out to share what they're working on. Where they can get feedback, learn new tricks and ideas, and connect with other innovators and thought leaders in their community.

If you're interested in participating or sharing your advanced gigabit enabled application, supporting the development of these technologies, or just learning what kind of exciting things are being created in your own backyard, check out our upcoming events list or get in touch with our team of organizers below. We also archive our past events for wider impact and to help give people an idea of what to expect.

About our format

Every third Wednesday of the month, our events begin with a half hour of networking and catching up with old acquaintances to help create a collegial and laid back atmosphere. The events may move around to make them accessible to more people and introduce attendees to the incredible startup and technology related spaces in their home town. Once everyone's well socialized and ready to go, one or our organizers will introduce the nights presenters.

The typical layout will begin with a demonstration by someone working on something we think is really awesome and worth introducing to our amazing guests, followed by a few minutes of questions. That'll then typically be followed by a 45 minute presentation and discussion with the night's featured presenter. In instances where the guest has a lot to say or we have a moderated panel, the demo will be skipped to allow some extra time.

Drinks and other refreshments served at the event are dependent on that particular location, and what kind of resources and restrictions are available. Some will allow things like alcohol and others won't, so plan accordingly.

The billion bits receipe

  • 30 Minutes - Social Refreshments
  • 5 Minutes - Opening Remarks
  • 45 Minutes - Guest Demo + Q&A (Optional)
  • 15 Minutes - Speaker Presentation
  • 30 Minutes - Discussion
